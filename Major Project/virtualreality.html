Virtual Reality - The immersive illusion

   Virtual reality is a computer-based idea. The idea is to replace reality with something new virtual computer-generated environment and it could be some game or some environment(as per the requirements). But the idea is to trick your brain into thinking that that's your new reality.

What is Virtual Reality?

   Virtual Reality (VR) is the use of computer technology to create a simulated environment. Unlike traditional user interfaces, VR places the user inside an experience. Instead of viewing a screen in front of them, users are immersed and able to interact with 3D worlds. Now a question will arise to your mind that how can we experience it? So we can experience it by wearing a VR headset(Like as shown above in the image) which is a head-mounted device(HMD). It provides Virtual reality to the wearer.

The need of VR or its applications

 The most common applications of VR are
  • Training Purposes like in military, sports, pilot training, and medical training.
  • Educational Purposes like the study of things which required 3D view.
  • Entertainment purpose, some game and video require 3D view or an environment in which the user can immerse.

Now, most of the people get confused with the difference between Augmented Reality and Virtual Reality, So let me clear you the differnce between both of them.

Virtual reality Vs. Augmented reality

   Instead of shutting out the physical world, Augmented reality only adds digital elements to a live view often by using camera on a smartphone. Examples of augmented reality experiences include Snapchat lenses and the game Pokemon Go.

   Augmented reality consists of both the real world and some artificial elements but Virtual reality only consists of artificial elements. For Example, As we have seen in the movie of Robert Downey(Tony stark): He always used to see something in the air and this what augmented reality is. Virtual reality is the use of computer technology to create a simulated environment. And Augmented reality is an enhanced version of reality created by the use of technology to add digital information on an image of something.
